Solvista · the loop's field log

A city that builds itself, one verified step at a time.

Solvista is a procedural cellular-automata city. It grows through a headless loop: each iteration is a fresh claude -p process with an empty context that reads the ledger, makes one improvement, verifies it with a numeric census and screenshot gates, commits, and exits — then the next one starts. No human in the loop. This page is the receipts.

Why later iterations cost more

Cost climbed after #195, and it decomposes cleanly. Iterations didn't get pricier per minute — $0.27/min before #195, $0.29/min after, essentially flat. They got longer: 18 → 40 min on average, as the easy wins ran out (reverts 7% → 13%) and the self-check protocol grew heavier. Runtime is what cost tracks (r = 0.80). Layered on top, each of those longer iterations re-reads a bigger pile of context (r = 0.69) — so the two compound.

Every iteration is a fresh process that re-reads the artifact plus the ledger it must obey, before it does any work. solvista.html grows gently; GROWTH.md is capped and stays flat; the SKILL.md skeleton barely moves; and the Laws section of SKILL.md is the wedge that runs away. Hover for the split.

The ledger that never forgets

That red wedge is the loop's institutional memory. When a lesson is learned the expensive way, the loop promotes it to a permanent “Laws the loop derived” list so it is never re-learned — and that list has grown from 0 laws (#76) to 95 (#270), now 66% of SKILL.md and read in full on every single run.

It exists because of a trade-off worth seeing plainly. GROWTH.md, the prose ledger, is budget-capped, so old entries rotate into an archive the loop stops reading — and it began re-deriving lessons it had already paid for. Promoting them to an uncapped list fixed the re-learning, but moved the growth to a file read every iteration: capping one ledger just pushed the cost into another. The clean fix isn't to un-cap GROWTH.md — it's to distil and cap the laws too, since many already supersede one another.
