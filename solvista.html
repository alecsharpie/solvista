Solvista

Founded 1974 on the California coast — and different every time. Streets, farms, rivers and rail all find their own way, hex by hex, toward the utopia the town always planned to be.

How it grows
  • Every city starts from a new seed: coastline, one to three rivers, woods, survey grid.
  • The survey runs on hexagons: streets follow the grain — east–west rows and the two hex diagonals.
  • Farm clusters feed the young town; the growing edge buys them out.
  • Homes appear where a street meets greenery; every parcel feels its neighbors' land value.
  • Busy corners become shops; shops raise towers; blocks vote themselves into districts.
  • Streets reach toward demand, bridging the river when they must.
  • The woods are logged at the edges, creep back, and sometimes burn.
  • Rain showers seed wildflower waves across the meadows.
  • The marine layer piles on the water at dawn and creeps a few blocks inland.
  • Fires jump block to block until the 1991 firehouse; the engine still patrols.
  • Schools follow the children; library, museum and capitol follow the decades.
  • Ball fields, stadiums, statues and plazas appear where the crowds already are.
  • Warehouses become lofts and far fields go solar once the rent says so.
  • From 1988 the monorail network opens line by line — each loop grows hex by hex toward high land value until it bites its own tail.
  • Cable cars creep down the shore the same way, one span at a time, wherever the view is earned.
